Hicks Law:

Company: Obsidan

https://obsidian.md

Hicks law states that the more simple the design is, the more likely the user is to stay and to more easily make decisions without being frustrated or unhappy with their experience. In Obsidians website, the first thing that you are able to find is a small description which is not too long, and a button to immediately download their software. This displays hicks law as there is a ver simple decision to make, to download the application or not! And nothing more, this is easy for the user to decide, or if they wish to look more into Obsidian, they are able to do so by just scrolling and learning more, with again just the decision to download the application or not. This is why I have chosen this website as my example for hicks law.

Rule Of Thrids:

Company: Porche

https://www.porsche.com/middle-east

The rule of thrids states that you can draw 2 lines horizontally and vertically evenly through an image to display 9 squares, and much of the 'weight' of the image is within the first third of the image. I chose Porche's website as my rule of thrids as many of their images displayed has their product (cars) many of their images are weighted on the left side of their images with their car, and not as much weight on the right hand side. This is why Porche's websites display rule of thrids well.

Proximity:

Compnay: Amazon

https://www.amazon.com

For proximity, I have chosen Amazons website. Proximity describes the closeness of objects from one to another, in Amazons websites all their products that they offer, are all in very close proximity to eachother, with nearly no whitespace at all. This provides the user with the idea that they sell much more than just one or more items, and helps the user understand that Amazon sells much more than what appears. This is why Amazon displays 'proximity' perfectly.
